My DNA

Medicine Meets Machine Intelligence

Dr. Kathryn Peper is a board-certified internal medicine physician who has spent her career at the intersection of science and education. After earning her B.A. in Biology and Psychology from Skidmore College — graduating with distinction and election to Phi Beta Kappa — she conducted immunology research at Yale School of Medicine before completing her M.D. at New York Medical College.

For 15 years she taught internal medicine at Morristown Memorial Hospital, earning recognition as a Fellow of the American College of Physicians for superior competence, professional accomplishment, and demonstrated leadership. Her private practice focuses on geriatric and end-of-life care.

Today, she channels that same drive for clarity into Short & Sweet AI, making artificial intelligence and futurist medicine accessible to everyone. She stays on the frontier by attending conferences in Exponential Medicine, Genomics, Medicine X, and MIT Hacking Medicine.
